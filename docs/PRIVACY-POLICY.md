## About This Notice

This Notice of Privacy Practices ("Notice") describes how your medical information may be used and shared, and how you can access this information. Please read it carefully.

When you visit a healthcare provider that uses Primus EHR, the provider creates a record of your care. This record includes your name, address, date of birth, diagnoses, treatments, medications, lab results, insurance details, and other information about your health. We call this your **Protected Health Information (PHI)**.

Federal law (the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, or **HIPAA**) requires us to:

- Keep your PHI private and secure
- Give you this Notice explaining our privacy practices
- Follow the terms of this Notice
- Tell you if a breach occurs that may have compromised your PHI

Your healthcare provider is the "covered entity" responsible for your care. Primus EHR, operated by Thinkitive Technologies, is a "business associate" that processes PHI on behalf of your provider under a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA).

## How We Use Your Information

### Treatment

We use your PHI to provide, coordinate, and manage your healthcare. Examples include:

- Your doctor reviews your medical history before an appointment
- A nurse records your vitals and updates your chart
- Your provider sends a referral to a specialist with relevant medical details
- Lab results are delivered to your provider through Primus EHR
- Prescriptions are sent electronically to your pharmacy

### Payment

We use your PHI to bill for services and process insurance claims. Examples include:

- Submitting claims to your health insurance plan
- Verifying your insurance eligibility before a visit
- Processing co-pays and patient balances
- Responding to insurance company requests for medical records related to a claim
- Sending statements for amounts you owe

### Healthcare Operations

We use your PHI for activities that support the quality and safety of your care. Examples include:

- Quality improvement and outcomes measurement
- Training clinical staff (with appropriate safeguards)
- Compliance audits and accreditation activities
- Reviewing the performance of providers and staff
- Business planning and management

## How We Share Your Information

### With Your Written Consent

For uses not described in this Notice, we will ask for your written permission (authorization) before using or sharing your PHI. You may cancel your authorization at any time in writing, but this will not affect information we already shared based on your prior permission.

We will always get your written consent before:

- Sharing your PHI for marketing purposes
- Selling your PHI
- Sharing psychotherapy notes (if applicable)

### Without Your Written Consent (As Permitted or Required by Law)

In certain situations, federal and state law allows or requires us to share your PHI without your written consent:

**Public Health Activities**
- Reporting communicable diseases to public health authorities
- Reporting births and deaths
- Reporting suspected child abuse or neglect
- Reporting adverse reactions to medications or medical devices to the FDA
- Notifying individuals who may have been exposed to a disease

**Health Oversight Activities**
- Sharing information with agencies that oversee the healthcare system, such as state health departments, CMS, or the HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR)

**Legal Proceedings**
- Responding to a court order or subpoena
- Providing information in connection with a legal dispute (with appropriate protections)

**Law Enforcement (Limited Circumstances)**
- Responding to a court order, warrant, or grand jury subpoena
- Helping identify or locate a suspect, fugitive, or missing person
- Reporting certain types of wounds or injuries as required by law
- Reporting a crime that occurs on the premises

**To Prevent a Serious Threat**
- Sharing information to prevent a serious and immediate threat to your health or safety, or the health or safety of others

**Workers' Compensation**
- Sharing information as required for workers' compensation claims

**Coroners, Medical Examiners, and Funeral Directors**
- Sharing information to identify a deceased person or determine cause of death

**Organ and Tissue Donation**
- Sharing information with organizations that handle organ, eye, or tissue donation and transplantation (if you are an organ donor)

**Military and Veterans**
- Sharing information with military command authorities if you are a member of the armed forces

**National Security**
- Sharing information with authorized federal officials for national security and intelligence activities

**Research**
- Sharing de-identified data (data that cannot be traced back to you) for approved research studies
- Sharing limited data sets under a data use agreement
- Sharing PHI for research approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) or Privacy Board

## Your Rights Under HIPAA

You have the following rights regarding your PHI. To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to your healthcare provider's Privacy Officer (contact information is at the end of this Notice).

### Right to Access Your Records

You have the right to look at or get a copy of your medical records. We will respond to your request within **30 days**. If we need more time, we will notify you in writing and complete your request within an additional 30 days.

- You may request records in paper or electronic format
- If you request electronic records, we will provide them in a commonly used electronic format (such as PDF or via the Primus patient portal)
- We may charge a reasonable fee for copying and mailing costs
- In rare cases, we may deny your request — if so, we will explain why and inform you of your right to appeal

### Right to Request an Amendment

If you believe your medical record contains an error, you have the right to ask us to correct it. Submit your request in writing and explain why you believe the information is incorrect.

- We will respond within **60 days**
- We may deny your request if the information was not created by us, is not part of our records, or is accurate and complete
- If we deny your request, we will explain why and you may submit a written statement of disagreement

### Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to receive a list of certain disclosures of your PHI that we have made. This list does not include disclosures made for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, or disclosures you authorized in writing.

- The list covers disclosures made in the **6 years** before your request
- We will respond within **60 days**
- The first request in a 12-month period is free; we may charge a reasonable fee for additional requests

### Right to Request Restrictions

You have the right to ask us to limit how we use or share your PHI for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations. You may also ask us to limit what we share with someone involved in your care (such as a family member).

- We are not required to agree to your request, except in one case: if you pay for a service entirely out of pocket, you have the right to ask us not to share information about that service with your health insurer, and we must honor that request
- If we agree to a restriction, we will follow it unless the information is needed to provide emergency treatment

### Right to Request Confidential Communications

You have the right to ask us to contact you in a specific way or at a specific location. For example, you may ask us to call you only at your work number or send mail to a P.O. Box instead of your home address.

- We will accommodate all reasonable requests
- You do not need to explain why you are making the request

### Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to request a paper copy of this Notice at any time, even if you previously agreed to receive it electronically. Ask your provider's front desk or contact the Privacy Officer.

## How We Protect Your Information

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your PHI. These include:

### Technical Safeguards

- **Encryption at rest:** All PHI stored in our databases is encrypted using AES-256-GCM, a standard used by banks and government agencies
- **Encryption in transit:** All data sent between your browser and our servers is protected with TLS 1.3 encryption
- **Access controls:** Every user has a unique login. Access is limited to only the information needed for their role (for example, front desk staff cannot see clinical notes)
- **Audit logging:** Every time someone views, creates, or changes your PHI, that action is recorded in a tamper-resistant audit log
- **Automatic session timeout:** If a user is inactive, they are automatically logged out to prevent unauthorized access
- **Security headers:** Our application enforces strict browser security policies (HSTS, Content Security Policy, X-Frame-Options) to prevent common web attacks

### Administrative Safeguards

- **Workforce training:** All staff with access to PHI complete HIPAA privacy and security training before accessing the system, and annually thereafter
- **Business Associate Agreements:** All vendors that may access PHI sign BAAs before receiving any data
- **Incident response plan:** We have a documented plan for responding to security incidents and breaches, including notification procedures
- **Risk assessments:** We conduct annual security risk assessments following NIST SP 800-30 methodology

### Physical Safeguards

- **Cloud infrastructure:** PHI is stored in AWS data centers that are SOC 2, ISO 27001, and HIPAA certified
- **No local storage:** PHI is not stored on individual workstations or laptops
- **Multi-tenant isolation:** Each healthcare organization's data is isolated in a separate database schema, preventing any cross-organization data access

## Data Retention

We retain your information according to the following schedule:

| Data Type | Retention Period | Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Medical records (encounters, diagnoses, medications, lab results) | Minimum **7 years** from date of last encounter, or longer if required by your state | Federal and state medical record retention laws |
| Audit logs (who accessed your records and when) | **6 years** | HIPAA requirement (45 CFR 164.530(j)) |
| Billing and claims records | **7 years** | IRS and CMS requirements |
| Account and login data | Until you request account deletion, plus **90 days** for processing | Operational need |
| Appointment history | **7 years** from appointment date | Consistent with medical record retention |
| Communication records (messages, SMS confirmations) | **3 years** | Operational need |

When records reach the end of their retention period, they are securely deleted using methods that make them unrecoverable.

If your state law requires a longer retention period than what is listed above, we will follow the longer requirement.

## Third-Party Services

Primus EHR integrates with the following third-party services. Each operates under a signed BAA and has its own privacy practices:

- **Amazon Web Services (AWS):** Hosts all application servers, databases, and file storage. AWS is HIPAA-eligible and operates under a BAA. Data is stored in the US.
- **Stripe:** Processes patient payments. Stripe is PCI DSS Level 1 certified and operates under a BAA. Stripe receives only billing-related information, not clinical records.
- **Twilio:** Sends appointment reminders, two-factor authentication codes, and other notifications via SMS. Twilio operates under a BAA. Messages contain minimal PHI (typically just appointment date/time and clinic name).
- **Keycloak:** Manages user authentication and role-based access. Keycloak runs within our AWS infrastructure and does not transmit PHI to external parties.
- **Quest Diagnostics:** Receives lab orders and returns lab results via secure HL7 interfaces.
- **Availity:** Transmits insurance claims and receives eligibility and remittance data via secure EDI connections.
- **ScriptSure:** Transmits electronic prescriptions, including controlled substances (EPCS), to pharmacies via the Surescripts network.

We do not sell your PHI to any third party. We do not use your PHI for targeted advertising.

## Children's Privacy

Primus EHR is used by healthcare providers to manage patient records, including records for patients under 13 years of age. The handling of children's health records is governed by HIPAA and applicable state laws.

- A parent or legal guardian may access a minor's health records through the Primus patient portal, subject to state law
- Certain state laws grant minors the right to consent to specific types of care (such as mental health or reproductive health) and restrict parental access to those records
- We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through our public website without parental consent

If you have questions about accessing your child's records, contact your healthcare provider.
